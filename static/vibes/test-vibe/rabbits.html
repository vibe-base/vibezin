All About Rabbits

Introduction to Rabbits

Rabbits are small mammals in the family Leporidae of the order Lagomorpha. There are eight different genera in the family classified as rabbits, including the European rabbit and cottontail rabbits. These furry creatures are known for their long ears, twitching noses, and strong hind legs.

Quick Facts

  • Rabbits are not rodents, but belong to the order Lagomorpha
  • A male rabbit is called a buck, a female is a doe, and a baby is a kit or kitten
  • Rabbits can live 8-12 years in captivity
  • They are crepuscular, meaning they are most active at dawn and dusk
  • A group of rabbits is called a colony or nest

Rabbit Breeds

There are over 50 recognized rabbit breeds around the world. Some popular pet rabbit breeds include:

  • Holland Lop - Known for their floppy ears and compact size
  • Netherland Dwarf - One of the smallest rabbit breeds
  • Rex - Famous for their plush, velvet-like fur
  • Flemish Giant - Can grow to over 20 pounds
  • Lionhead - Recognized by the mane of fur around their head

Rabbit Diet

Rabbits are herbivores with a specialized digestive system. Their diet should consist of:

  • Hay - Should make up 80-90% of a rabbit's diet
  • Fresh vegetables - Leafy greens like kale, romaine lettuce, and herbs
  • Limited pellets - High-quality, timothy-based pellets
  • Occasional treats - Small amounts of fruits like apple or banana

Fresh water should always be available to rabbits.

Rabbit Behavior

Rabbits communicate through body language and sounds. Some common behaviors include:

  • Binkying - Jumping and twisting in the air, a sign of happiness
  • Flopping - Falling to their side dramatically, indicating they feel safe and relaxed
  • Thumping - Stomping their hind feet to alert others of danger
  • Purring/teeth grinding - Gentle grinding means contentment, loud grinding can indicate pain
  • Chinning - Rubbing their chin on objects to mark territory
